The Case Against Coke

By Alexander Davis

June 5, 2022 | Estimated reading time: 1 minute

You're probably familiar with the popular soft drink Coca-Cola. Coke claims that its products aren't addictive, but a quick Google search[1] [2] [3] proves otherwise. People range from seldom consuming it to drinking extreme amounts such as ten a day. This article intends to be a wake-up call to those heavily consuming coke.

#

Health Problems


What exactly makes Coke bad? For starters, daily diet soda drinkers have a 48% increase in heart attack and stroke risk.[4]

In addition, sugary sodas can cause weight gain, insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes, leptin resistance, heart disease, cancer, tooth decay, and even dementia.[5]

What if I drink zero calorie sodas like Coke Zero? In order to maintain the same taste but eliminate calories, Coke Zero has to employ many artificial sweeteners, including Aspartame and acesulfame potassium.[6] [7] The effects of Aspartame are controversial, and there is no agreement on whether Aspartame is "good" or "bad" for you.[8]

#

Conclusion


Ultimately, it is up to you to decide whether you want to and how often you want to drink Coke; however, your health and body will thank you in the long run if you choose to drink it infrequently. It is advisable to avoid Coke because it will only save you money and health complications down the road.
